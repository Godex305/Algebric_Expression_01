# Slide2AimAndWorking

So, our aim in the today's video is to determine the expansion of the algebric expansion a plus b whole squared.
For this, Let's consider the term a plus b whole squared. Which can be rewritten as a plus b times a plus b.
Now, we will use the fudamental distributive law to simplify further.
As we can see in the next step that a is distributed over a plus b then putting the plus sign b is distributed over a plus b.
Then, by solving further we get a times a plus a times b plus b times a and b times b.
Which can be rewritten as a squared plus a times b plus a times b plus b squared.
Finally, By adding the like terms we get, a squared plus 2 times ab plus b squared.
Hence, we are done with the expansion.


# Slide3ConclisionSlide
At the last we get that a plus b whole squared is decomposed into a squared plus 2 times ab plus b squared. Which is the fundamental algebric identity.
Now, let us try one application of the proved identity with the help of an example.
The examples is asking us to expand 5x plus 3 whole squared using the proved identity.
We can do it easily by replacing a by 5x and b by 3 in the given identity. Simple calculations will lead to the required.
